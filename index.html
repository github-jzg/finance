Strategy

Separate retirement readiness, asset location, and tax optimization. First build a taxable Treasury runway and bond bucket so spending does not depend on selling stocks in a bad market. Then use the low-income years between retirement and RMDs to choose between Roth conversions and long-term capital-gains harvesting.

Why

The Treasury and bond buckets protect lifestyle spending. Roth conversions can reduce future RMD pressure from tax-deferred accounts. Capital-gains harvesting can reset taxable basis when income is low enough to use favorable federal long-term capital-gains brackets.

How to read this

Dollar action is the portfolio move. The three strategy columns are alternatives, not commands to do all at once. Pick the column that best matches that year's tax bracket, healthcare subsidy or IRMAA exposure, and need for taxable cash.

Taxable bridge

Use non-retirement accounts for the age 54-60 bridge and early retirement cash flow. Hold VUSXX/VMFXX/SGOV/T-bills for runway, keep high-basis VOO/VTI lots available for optional cash, and harvest gains only when the tax bracket makes sense.

Tax-deferred

Treat IRA/401k assets as the RMD-pressure bucket. It is a good home for BND/VGIT/Treasuries, and it is the source account for planned Roth conversions of VOO/VTI or bond ETF shares during low-income years.

Roth growth

Use Roth space for the longest-horizon growth assets after conversions, usually broad stock ETFs such as VOO/VTI. Spend this bucket late unless taxes, estate goals, or risk control argue otherwise.

Unvested stock

Track it for net worth, but exclude it from cash runway and defensive bucket math until it vests and is liquid. Once vested, decide whether it becomes spending runway, taxable equity, or tax-payment cash.

Rules to model with your CPA

  • Use taxable cash/treasuries first where it preserves flexibility before age 59½.
  • Keep the buckets distinct: taxable funds the bridge, tax-deferred manages RMD risk, and Roth holds last-dollar growth.
  • Do not count unvested stock as runway until it has vested and can actually be sold.
  • Harvest long-term capital gains up to the 0% federal bracket when taxable income allows.
  • Convert traditional IRA/401k dollars to Roth in low-income years, but watch Medicare IRMAA once Medicare age approaches.
  • Plan RMDs before they start so tax-deferred accounts do not force oversized taxable income later.
